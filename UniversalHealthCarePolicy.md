# Universal Health Care Payment Policy (IOWB Model)

## Policy Statement

All health care services, regardless of the current system or provider, shall be paid from the Internet Order World Bank (IOWB). This ensures universal access, equity, and administrative simplicity.

## Key Principles

- **Universal Coverage:** Every individual is entitled to health care services without direct out-of-pocket payment at the point of care.
- **Centralized Payment:** All health care providers bill the IOWB for services rendered. Individuals do not pay providers directly.
- **Funding:** The IOWB is funded through progressive taxes, digital transaction fees, or global contributions, replacing private insurance premiums and most out-of-pocket costs.
- **Order Processing Fee:** A 2% order processing fee is applied to all BS-Molecule transactions. This fee is paid by the Selling Entity and contributes directly to the sustainability of the IOWB system.
- **Value Added Tax (VAT):** A flat 10% VAT is applied to all BS-Molecule transactions, regardless of entity size or type. The VAT is collected by the Selling Entity and remitted to the IOWB, ensuring symmetry and equal contribution from all participants.
- **Provider Choice:** Patients may choose any licensed provider or hospital.
- **Cost Controls:** The IOWB negotiates prices for drugs, devices, and services, and reduces administrative overhead.
- **Equity:** The system is designed to reduce disparities in access and outcomes.

## Implementation Steps

1. Establish the IOWB as the sole payer for all health care services.
2. Transition all provider billing to the IOWB system.
3. Phase out direct patient billing and private insurance for covered services.
4. Implement funding mechanisms to ensure sustainability and fairness, including:
   - Require all Selling Entities to add a 10% VAT to every BS-Molecule transaction invoice.
   - Ensure VAT collected is remitted to the IOWB on a regular basis.
   - Allow input tax credits for VAT paid on business inputs, if applicable.
5. Monitor and adjust for quality, efficiency, and equity.

## Universal Health Bill of Rights

Every individual is guaranteed the following rights under the Universal Health Care Payment Policy:

1. **Right to Health Care:** Access to comprehensive, timely, and high-quality health care services without discrimination.
2. **Right to Choice:** Freedom to choose any licensed health care provider or facility.
3. **Right to Privacy:** Protection of personal health information and medical records.
4. **Right to Informed Consent:** Clear, understandable information about treatment options, risks, and benefits, and the right to accept or refuse care.
5. **Right to Equity:** Equal treatment and access regardless of income, status, geography, or background.
6. **Right to Preventive Care:** Access to preventive services, screenings, and health education.
7. **Right to Emergency Care:** Immediate care in emergencies, regardless of ability to pay or status.
8. **Right to Appeal:** The ability to appeal decisions or denials of care through a fair and transparent process.
9. **Right to Participation:** The opportunity to participate in health policy development and provide feedback on the system.
10. **Right to Accountability:** Assurance that the system and providers are held accountable for quality, safety, and ethical standards.

This policy formalizes a single-payer, universal health care model with payment centralized through the IOWB, ensuring health care is a right for all.
